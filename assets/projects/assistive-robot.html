Assistive Navigation Robot

A six-month team project combining spatial detection, audible warnings, and separation alerts to support safer walking for a visually impaired user.

CONTEXTUniversity Coding Course
TIMELINESix-month design cycle
COLLABORATIONTeam of five
THE CHALLENGE

Create an affordable assistive device that could help a visually impaired person recognize nearby obstacles and alert a trusted contact if the user became separated from the robot. The project required our team to integrate code, sensors, electronics, mechanical assembly, and a simple user interaction into one working prototype.

MY ROLE

I worked on a five-person team through a full six-month design and build cycle. My responsibilities included assembly, component integration, MATLAB development, troubleshooting, test planning, and helping translate the accessibility concept into a functioning prototype.

MATLABSensor integrationCOTS hardwareMechanical assemblyRapid prototyping
ENGINEERING PROCESS

How the work moved from problem to functioning result.

01

Define the user need

We chose an assistive walking concept rather than a general demonstration robot, which gave every design decision a clear purpose.

02

Integrate sensing and feedback

The robot used spatial detection to identify nearby obstacles and produce an audible warning as the user approached them.

03

Add separation monitoring

A tether or “leash” provided another safety layer. If the connection pulled away, the system initiated a message to a designated contact.

04

Test and refine

We repeatedly adjusted code, wiring, sensor behavior, and the physical layout until the hardware and software behaved as one system.

OUTCOME

The prototype completed the required functions, and our team earned an A on the final project. More importantly, it showed me how mechanical systems, sensors, and software must be designed together around the person using them.

WHAT I LEARNED

This was my steepest coding learning curve. I did not begin the project as a confident programmer, but six months of MATLAB development and hardware troubleshooting made me much more comfortable learning unfamiliar technical tools and contributing across disciplines.
