Lavo Bot Prototype Development

Hands-on research, testing, and prototype iteration on a pressure-washing robot that became my introduction to engineering product development.

CONTEXTLUCID Bots
TIMELINEEarly engineering experience
COLLABORATIONDirect collaboration with company leadership and engineers
THE CHALLENGE

Help turn an ambitious pressure-washing robot concept into a more capable, testable product through hands-on prototype work, research, and repeated mechanical and system-level iteration.

MY ROLE

I supported testing, assembly, troubleshooting, research, and design exploration while working closely with the CEO and engineering team on a high-priority prototype. Because the project was personally important to company leadership, I was fortunate to see the intensity and persistence required to develop a product from an early idea.

ENGINEERING PROCESS

How the work moved from problem to functioning result.

01

Understand the system

Opening the machine and tracing its mechanisms, wiring, drive components, cleaning hardware, and controls taught me to think in complete systems.

02

Build and test

I participated in hands-on assembly and testing rather than observing from the sidelines.

03

Research alternatives

We considered component choices, layouts, operating behavior, maintenance, and practical user needs while the concept evolved.

04

Iterate from evidence

Prototype failures and test results were treated as information that guided the next change.

OUTCOME

The Lavo Bot project was my introduction to real engineering development and the experience that got me hooked. It showed me that engineering is not one perfect idea—it is a cycle of building, testing, learning, and improving.

WHAT I LEARNED

Working close to a passionate product leader showed me how much ownership matters. The best prototypes improve quickly when the team is willing to inspect failures honestly, change direction, and keep testing.
