Before the Recordable

A proactive construction safety concept that turns fast QR-based near-miss reports into leading indicators, corrective actions, and visible trends.

CONTEXTGaylor Electric Capstone
TIMELINESummer 2026
COLLABORATIONCapstone project
THE CHALLENGE

Near misses often contain the earliest evidence of a dangerous pattern, but reporting friction and disconnected follow-up can cause those signals to disappear. The goal was to make reporting easier and turn individual observations into useful leading indicators.

MY ROLE

I developed the concept for a QR-code reporting workflow and a management dashboard that could organize hazard categories, locations, response times, corrective actions, and recurring patterns. I then turned the idea into a structured capstone presentation focused on practical jobsite adoption.

QR reportingDashboard designProcess mappingSafety analyticsPresentation
ENGINEERING PROCESS

How the work moved from problem to functioning result.

01

Reduce reporting friction

A QR code gives field personnel a direct path to a short report instead of requiring a complicated process.

02

Structure the data

Consistent fields make it possible to compare events by hazard type, location, timing, and response status.

03

Reveal weak signals

A dashboard converts isolated observations into trends that safety leaders can act on before an incident becomes recordable.

04

Close the loop

Response tracking and corrective-action visibility show whether a reported concern was acknowledged, assigned, and resolved.

OUTCOME

Before the Recordable reframes near-miss reporting as an operational feedback system rather than a compliance task. The concept is designed to help project teams see risk sooner, respond faster, and learn across multiple reports.

WHAT I LEARNED

A useful safety solution must respect field reality. The strongest concept is not the one with the most features—it is the one workers will actually use and leaders can act on quickly.
