Modular Electrical Skid: Model to Field

A fast Revit ramp-up on a multi-hundred-million-dollar data center project, carrying modular skid work from submittal and modeling through fabrication and a field lift.

CONTEXTGaylor Electric
TIMELINESummer 2026
COLLABORATIONVDC, prefab, engineering, and field teams
THE CHALLENGE

Rapidly become productive in professional Revit and VDC workflows while supporting modular electrical skids on a multi-hundred-million-dollar data center project. The design had to move cleanly from submittal information to coordinated model content, published sheets, fabrication, and field installation.

MY ROLE

I created and revised skid-related model content, interpreted project prints and submittals, checked dimensions and constructability, supported drawing production, and used Revit, Inventor, AutoCAD, Bluebeam Revu, Autodesk Construction Cloud, and Evolve throughout the workflow. I also supported the calculations and planning associated with lifting the completed assembly.

Autodesk RevitInventorAutoCADBluebeam RevuAutodesk Construction CloudEvolve
ENGINEERING PROCESS

How the work moved from problem to functioning result.

01

Translate source information

I converted 2D prints, AutoCAD details, equipment information, and existing plans into coordinated 3D content.

02

Design for fabrication

The model had to represent how the frame, equipment, mounting conditions, and routed systems would actually be assembled in the prefab environment.

03

Document and coordinate

I supported the path from submittal review to published sheets while checking dimensions, installation details, and potential conflicts before field work.

04

Connect calculations to execution

The project became real when I watched a skid influenced by my modeling and calculations being lifted roughly 50 feet into position on the jobsite.

OUTCOME

This project compressed a major learning curve into one internship. I went from learning company Revit standards to contributing on a high-value project and then seeing coordinated digital work become a fabricated and installed assembly.

WHAT I LEARNED

The most rewarding part of VDC is the closed loop between model, shop, and field. A clean model is not the finish line—the real test is whether the information supports safe fabrication, predictable installation, and confident field execution.
