Sherpa Cleaning Drone Production

Electromechanical assembly and troubleshooting for an industrial exterior-cleaning platform.

RoleRobotics Engineering Technician at LUCID Bots
ToolsMechanical assembly, wiring, production testing, troubleshooting, quality checks
FocusRobotics • Manufacturing • Electromechanical

Challenge

Support reliable production of a complex cleaning drone that combines a carbon-fiber frame, motors, power electronics, communications hardware, sensors, and fluid-delivery components.

Approach

Built full systems from mechanical subassemblies through wiring, setup, inspection, and final testing. Diagnosed fit, routing, damaged-component, and quality-control issues while communicating recurring concerns to engineering and production leads.

Outcome

Helped move the team from a backlog of roughly 50 units toward on-time production while gaining hands-on experience with complete electromechanical systems.

What I Learned

  • Reliable production depends on consistent routing, fastening, inspection, and documentation.
  • Recurring build problems should be surfaced as process-improvement opportunities, not treated as isolated fixes.
  • A complete-system viewpoint helps connect individual assembly steps to final product performance.
