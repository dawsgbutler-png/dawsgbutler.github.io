Custom Skid Lift Planning

Engineering calculations connected directly to a successful field lift.

RoleCalculation and project-engineering support
ToolsDrawing review, component data, weight estimates, load-path reasoning, field coordination
FocusField Engineering • Load Planning • Constructability

Challenge

Support the safe movement of a custom modular skid by translating model and component information into practical lift-planning inputs.

Approach

Used project dimensions and equipment information to support expected-weight calculations and lift assumptions, then compared the planned configuration with the actual field setup before observing execution.

Outcome

Connected analytical work to a real construction operation and saw the custom skid lifted into position as planned.

What I Learned

  • Field calculations must be traceable to drawings, equipment data, and clearly stated assumptions.
  • A technically correct result still needs to be communicated in a form the project team can use.
  • Watching the lift provided immediate feedback on how design, rigging, access, and field conditions interact.
ApplicationCustom modular skid
MethodCalculated planning inputs
