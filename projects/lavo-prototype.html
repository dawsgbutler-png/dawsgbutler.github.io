Lavo Bot Prototype Development

Hands-on prototype work for a high-productivity pressure-washing robot.

RolePrototype assembly and electromechanical troubleshooting
ToolsMechanical fabrication, wiring, motors, controls, rapid iteration, hand and power tools
FocusPrototyping • Robotics • Product Development

Challenge

Develop and refine an early ground-based pressure-washing robot around a large surface-cleaner assembly while packaging drive, control, and fluid-handling components into a workable prototype.

Approach

Contributed to prototype assembly, component placement, wiring, fit checks, and troubleshooting. The open architecture made it possible to test ideas quickly and revise the arrangement as the product concept evolved.

Outcome

Supported early development work that helped turn a pressure-washing concept into a functional robotic platform.

What I Learned

  • Prototype layouts should prioritize access, testability, and fast modification before final packaging.
  • Mechanical, electrical, and fluid systems have to be considered together from the beginning.
  • Hands-on prototype work exposes integration problems that are easy to miss in isolated component design.
